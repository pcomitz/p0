George Shuffler

Guitar player for the Stanley Brothers. George was an active plater from 1950 to 1967. The Stanley brothers were an iconic band that was formed by brothers Ralph and Carter Stanley in the late 1940s. The work of the Stanley Brothers was a prototype for American bluegrass. Thier influence remains prevalent today.

George Shuffler was the guitar player in the band. When George joined the band in 1950, guitar was primarily a rhythm instrument. The role of guitar was was to play chords, supporting melody instruments such as fiddle and mandolin. Shuffler created a style that transformed the guitar from a rhythm only instrument, to a rhythm and melody instrument. Shuffler's work was based largely on technique known as crosspicking. Crosspicking emulates thesound of a banjo roll (notes played in sequence, usually eight notes) on the guitar.
