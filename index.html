This short project explores several famous Flatpick guitar payers. Flatpicking refers to a guitar playing style based on the use of a flatpick, sometimes referred to as a plectrum. Flatpicking is usually associated with acoustic and bluegrass music. However flatpicking is prevalent in many other forms of music such as roots, americana, blues, and other styles. In this site we take a quick look at 3 of foundational flatpicking guitar players. These players established the style. This is style is very popular and widely practiced today. In some ways all flatpickers are, consciously or unconsciously copying the work of these players.

Flatpicker Description Time Period
George Shuffler Played with the Stanley Brothers. A pioneer in the movement of flatpick guitar from a from a rhythm instrument to a full fledged melody instrument. 1950 - 1967
Clarence White Originally played with the famed Bluegrass Band The Kentucky Colonels . Later in his career he joined the American rock and roll band The Byrds. 1954-1973
Tony Rice A legendary flatpicker. Many would say that Tony Rice is the standard from which all modern flatpicking guitar is judged. 1970-2013
